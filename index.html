Hi Y'all!

Welcome to my website. I started this project after a few men at a hackathon ticked me off.

Let me backtrack for a second. A hackathon is a 24 hour coding-fest hosted for people of all experience levels. With a growing interest in code (and frankly the free giveaways involved), I, too, signed up for this hackathon. Shortly after my friends and I arrived, the three of us met a group of hackers who were interested in having us join their team. As our discussions continued, I realized the group of hackers wanted my friends to join their team, but not me. They weren't even talking to me. But, why?

I was the only female in the group. In fact, I was one of the few females in the entire room. My friends were not more experienced than myself, they were not more charasmatic than myself, they were not more interested than myself. Call it jumping to conclusions or a bias or really whatever you want, but the fact that I can not confidently eliminate my gender as a reason for my exclusion is a mark of concern not only for myself, but also for a society that prides itself on freedom and equality.

As I walked home angrily and teary-eyed from the hackathon, I promised myself I would learn how to code. With the help of Codeacademy, Stack Overflow and my less mysogynistic friends, I did just that. I thank the men who tried to shut me out because you helped me realize that shutting me out doesn't mean shutting me up. While this isn't a complex website by any means, it speaks volumes about the power of relentless determination. To women around the world I say, dont shut up. We are capable of anything we set our minds to.

Yours truly,

Alena
